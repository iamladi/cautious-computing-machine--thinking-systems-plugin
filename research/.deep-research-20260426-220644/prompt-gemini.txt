### A. Framing tactics — corroborate, extend, or challenge Hughes's framework

A1. Cross-validate the **5 statement classes** (Pacing, Alignment, Resonance, Concession, Presupposition) and **5 question classes** (Directional, Assumptive, Elicitation, Reframe, Diagnostic) against the broader literature: Goffman, Lakoff, Tversky-Kahneman, Cialdini (especially *Pre-Suasion*), Voss (*Never Split the Difference*), Bandler-Grinder NLP work, Fisher-Ury (*Getting to Yes*), Schein (humble inquiry), Boyd (OODA Orient stage), academic conversation analysis. Where do other traditions corroborate the move? Where do they name a *different* primitive Hughes is missing?

A2. Identify framing tactics in the literature that **Hughes does not cover** but are well-attested. Candidates to evaluate (not exhaustive): anchoring, contrast principle, accusation audit (Voss), labeling vs. mirroring split, "no-oriented" questions, scarcity / loss-frame, identity-stake framing, temporal framing (10-year-from-now lens), priming/pre-suasion, Socratic elenchus, narrative reframing.

A3. The **recovery loop** ("read the miss → release → shift class → redeploy"). How well-supported is this in the literature? Are there better-developed recovery models (e.g., Voss's "tactical empathy" recovery, Argyris's double-loop, Schein's Process Consultation)?

A4. **Defensive read.** How does someone *detect* frames being installed on them in real time? Tools: Goffman's "frame breaks", Schein's left-hand column, ladder-of-inference (already in plugin). Phase 2 must take a clear position on whether defensive framing belongs in the same skill as offensive framing or in separate skills.

A5. **Ethics.** Where is the line between honest frame-setting and manipulation? Hughes's claim that the doctor/boss/parent are not consciously manipulating is empirically defensible but risks moral cover. Take a position the plugin can adopt without becoming a manipulation-coaching tool.
